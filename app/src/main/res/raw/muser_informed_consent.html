Researchers at the University of South Florida (USF) study many topics. To do this, we need the help of people who agree to take part in a research study. The person who is in charge of this research study is Dr. Hongdao Meng. This study is sponsored by the University of South Florida Strategic Investment Program

Purpose of the Study

The purpose of this study is to develop a prototype music listening application that helps researchers better understand the music listening behavior of family caregivers of persons living with dementia, including what type of music and which pieces of music were played on a daily basis and for how long. This data will be used to inform the design of music interventions for persons living with dementia to be used by caregivers.

How do I participate?

If you take part in this study, your music listening information will be automatically collected by the MUSER Android application. You will also be asked (via email) to participate in a few online surveys about how and when you play music for yourself and/or your loved one. Each survey will take about 2 minutes to complete, and you will be asked to complete approximately 14 surveys over a period of two weeks (one per day). You may also be prompted occasionally to provide brief information about your listening (e.g., "Did you listen to music during the previous two hours?").

There is no compensation for participating in this study. There is no cost to participate.

Privacy and Confidentiality

We will store your data securely in a database accessible only to the research team. Your email address will be used for communication about this study (e.g., a link to online surveys) and will not be used for any other purpose.

We may publish and share what we learn from this study. If we do, we will not let anyone know your name. We will not share anything else that would let people know who you are.
